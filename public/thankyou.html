Web

Web design is one of the skills I have acquired in the last 2-3 years. I also learnt about web development. That makes my Designer profile more solution oriented because I can understand better the technical side of the project so I know well what kind of deliverables will be necessary and can work focused to get them efficiently.

I have worked with different clients in a variety of projects. Some examples are complete e-commerce sites, small touch ups in few hours or urgent one-week 24/7 projects. I am flexible in the level of detail required, the complexity needed or the style desired.

My main focus as a web designer is working efficiently with clear communication to get the client satisfaction.

UX / UI

Designing interfaces or elements for user interaction is great. I love to learn, and so that I love user experience design. This kind of design is always about learning how your specific group of users interacts with the product and how to get it better.

I constantly read about experience design, my favourite aspect of User Experience design is the psychologic side, understanding how the composition of the visual elements makes the user behave in certain way is something amazing.

I have developed some app interfaces and also applied this knowledge in web design.

Product

Physical Product Design has been always one of my passions. Since I was a child I have been always playing around with different materials, building small toys or modifying machines to build others.

With the age of 10 I took part of a wood workshop for a year where I developed some skills that afterwards gave me the ability to always keep in touch with the crafted side of the manufacturing process. I love the steps of the design process where you model the real products and touch the materials itself.

In 2011 I won an international contest combining my structural design skills with my craft skills. My wood sticks bridge suported more than 1116kg of load. The complete bridge with the base included only weight 4kg and is still entire and exposed in the hall of the University Miguel Hernández in Elche, Spain.
